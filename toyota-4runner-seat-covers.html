Last updated April 2026 · 9 min read

The 4Runner has been Toyota’s do-everything SUV for decades. It hauls families, runs trails, tows boats, and somehow keeps going past 300,000 miles. But the factory seats don’t age as gracefully as the drivetrain. Whether you’ve got a 4th gen with fading leather or a 5th gen TRD Pro with cloth that shows every stain, seat covers are a smart investment. Here’s what we recommend after testing the major brands.

4Runner Seat Cover Tips

  • 3rd row seats: If you have the 3rd row, finding covers is tricky. Most brands only make front and 2nd row. Bartact covers the main seating areas; for the 3rd row, a universal bench cover is usually the best bet.
  • 4th gen vs 5th gen: The seats are completely different. Don’t mix them up when ordering. 4th gen (2003-2009) options are more limited but Coverking and CalTrend still make them.
  • Heated seats: If you have the heated seat option, make sure covers are compatible. Thick covers can reduce heat transfer. Bartact and Coverking both confirm heated-seat compatibility on their 4Runner covers.
  • Dog owners: The 4Runner is basically the official dog-hauler SUV. Consider a rear cargo liner too—check options on Amazon.
