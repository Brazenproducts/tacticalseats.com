Best Toyota Tacoma Seat Covers for 2026

Last updated April 2026 · 10 min read

The Tacoma is one of the most capable mid-size trucks ever made, and most owners actually use theirs. That means muddy boots, sandy wetsuits, greasy tools, and the occasional dog. Factory seats don’t stand a chance. We tested tactical and heavy-duty covers on 2nd gen (2005-2015) and 3rd gen (2016-2023) Tacomas to find what’s worth buying.

Tacoma Generation Breakdown

2nd Gen (2005-2015)

These Tacomas are getting older, which means the factory seats have more wear. The good news is that aftermarket support is excellent. Bartact offers covers for the full 2005-2015 range, including both Access Cab and Double Cab configurations. If you’re keeping your 2nd gen on the road (and you should—they’re bulletproof), invest in covers now before the foam starts showing.

Shop at Bartact →

3rd Gen (2016-2023)

The 3rd gen Tacoma has a more modern interior with different seat bolster shapes and side airbag locations. Make sure any covers you buy are specifically designed for the 3rd gen—2nd gen covers won’t fit right and could interfere with airbag deployment. Bartact, Coverking, and Rough Country all make generation-specific options.

Tacoma-Specific Tips

  • The Tacoma driver’s seat sits lower than most trucks. Make sure covers don’t add too much bulk or you’ll feel like you’re sitting in a hole.
  • If you have the power driver’s seat, check that covers don’t interfere with the adjustment controls.
  • TRD Pro models have different headrests—verify compatibility before ordering.
  • Rear seat fold-down functionality should still work with covers installed. RealTruck has a good compatibility checker for this.
